## Overview
The core power nation of the Confederacy. Edom maintains dominant political presence across continents, often embedding leadership and counselors within other nations’ governance.

## Structure
- Central ruling council with regional governors.
- Diplomatic corps placed in allied nations.
- Merchant-administrative class that controls trade terms.
- Military command integrated with confederate enforcement.

## Goals
- Sustain confederate dominance and stability.
- Expand influence through governance placement and treaty leverage.
- Maintain suppression of the Twelve Tribes.
- Preserve Edom’s internal hierarchy and elite continuity.

## Methods
- Political appointment networks.
- Treaty-based economic coercion.
- Controlled advancement opportunities for non-Edom populations.
- Enforcement partnerships with other confederate militaries.

## Relationships
- **Amalek:** sub-lineage and shadow-influence nation; politically intertwined.
- **Other Confederacy Nations:** dominant partner; sets trade and policy baseline.
- **Twelve Tribes:** primary suppressed class and labor base in multiple regions.
