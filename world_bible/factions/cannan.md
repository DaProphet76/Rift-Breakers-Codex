## Overview
A lower-ranked confederate nation that functions as the merchant and labor buffer within the confederate hierarchy. Canaan is positioned above the Twelve Tribes socially while remaining subordinate to the other confederate nations.

## Structure
- Merchant houses licensed by confederate authorities.
- Local administrators managing commerce and labor allocation.
- Security bureaus with limited authority.

## Goals
- Maintain elevated status above the Twelve Tribes.
- Secure economic opportunities through confederate alignment.
- Avoid being fully displaced by higher confederate nations.

## Methods
- Small business licensing and controlled opportunity allocation.
- Labor brokerage and contract enforcement.
- Sale of lower-tier weapons and tools under restriction.

## Relationships
- **Edom:** dependent partnership for market access.
- **Ishmael:** trade interdependence.
- **Twelve Tribes:** hierarchical oppression through restricted access and labor control.
