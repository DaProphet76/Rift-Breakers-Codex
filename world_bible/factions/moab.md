## Overview
A confederate war-nation defined by disciplined military doctrine and resource-driven industry. Moab’s strategic identity centers on efficiency, doctrine, and controlled aggression.

## Structure
- Militarized governance with civilian production guilds.
- Officer-caste influence on policy.
- Resource extraction and manufacturing syndicates.

## Goals
- Maintain dominance through military credibility.
- Profit through low-cost production and controlled export.
- Preserve confederate alignment while advancing Moab’s leverage.

## Methods
- Doctrine-driven training and warcraft education.
- Resource consolidation and industrial scaling.
- Export of standardized goods to allied nations at profit margins.

## Relationships
- **Ammon:** parallel nation with shared origin narratives and competitive alignment.
- **Edom/Amalek:** policy alignment through treaties; occasional friction over autonomy.
- **Twelve Tribes:** used as labor under controlled economic access.
